# Notes from College

*Things college quietly taught me*

* Don't expect the world to be kind. Be kind anyway, but learn where to stop giving more than you have.

* Chaos looks exciting from far away. Living in it all the time is overrated.

* Time keeps moving no matter what you choose to do. So you might as well spend it on things that feel worth it (to you).

* Balance matters more than grind. Some of my best work happened after I stepped away and did something fun.

* No one really pulls you up. Most growth is self-driven. If someone genuinely helps or pushes you forward, don't lose them.

* Quality over quantity, in work, in interests, and even in people. Fewer things done well felt better than doing everything halfway.

* Being unheard isn't always about you. Sometimes it's the space, the timing, or the people, and that's okay to walk away from.

* People talk, change their minds, forget, and move on. That includes how they think about you, and even how you think about yourself.

* Everyone moves on a different timeline. Chasing someone else's version of progress gets tiring fast, and often isn't meant for you anyway.
