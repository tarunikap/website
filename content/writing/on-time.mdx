# on time

***some books and shows mess with time***  
***i blink and suddenly i'm older***

Arthur Read stayed in third grade forever.  
I'm not exaggerating. That kid did not move. At all.

I watched *Arthur* as a child. Then I grew up. Changed schools. Finished school. Started college. Nearly finished college. And somehow, Arthur was still in third grade, worrying about homework and bike rides like nothing had happened.

That realisation hit me out of nowhere and felt absurd. Like how did *I* age this much and he didn't move an inch?

There was something comforting about that. Time froze for him. You could disappear for years, come back, and everything would still be familiar. Same characters. Same problems. Same world.

And then there are stories that do the opposite. Like *The Clifton Chronicles*, which starts in early 20th-century England and quietly walks you through decades of lives, choices, mistakes, wars, cities, and generations. You don't just read those books. You move through time with them.

Both mess with my head in different ways.

One makes me realise how fast life moved while I wasn't paying attention. The other makes me feel like I've lived more years than I actually have.

Arthur's probably still worrying about homework.  
I somehow moved on to worrying about time.
