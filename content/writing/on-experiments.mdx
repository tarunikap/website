# on experiments

***some ideas look better***  
***before you try them***

Every now and then, you feel the urge to start something new.

A hobby. A plan. A skill that suddenly feels important.

In your head, it is smooth. You imagine the version of you who does it well. Who enjoys it. Who sticks with it.

So you begin.

The first few days feel heavy. A little overwhelming. There is too much to learn. Too many mistakes.

That is usually where interest fades.

The unstarted version felt lighter. It did not demand repetition. It did not expose how average you are at the beginning.

If you stay with it a little longer, something shifts. What felt overwhelming starts to make sense. Small progress shows up. The work becomes interesting again, but in a quieter way.

That part only happens if you keep going past the first drop in interest. If you stop there, you never see it.

Then the cycle repeats. The excitement fades again. The effort feels heavy again. And again, you keep going.

It looks like the same loop. But it is not the same place.

It moves outward.  
Like a spiral, slowly toward something.

Interest gets you to start.  
Staying is something else.
